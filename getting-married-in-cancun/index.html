How to Get Married in Cancun

Everything you need to know about legal and symbolic ceremonies in the Mexican Caribbean.

Choose Your Ceremony Path

Symbolic Ceremony

Perfect for couples who want to focus on the celebration without the paperwork in Mexico. Usually performed by a non-denominational minister.

  • ✓ No legal documentation required in Mexico.
  • ✓ Fully customizable script and rituals.
  • ✓ Lower cost and administrative effort.
  • ✕ Not legally binding; must marry in home country.
Learn More

Legal Ceremony

A Civil Marriage recognized globally. Performed by a Mexican Justice of the Peace in Spanish with translation provided.

  • ✓ Legally recognized in your home country.
  • ✓ Authentically Mexican judicial process.
  • ✓ Official marriage certificate provided.
  • ✕ Requires strict documentation and blood tests.

Legal Requirements Timeline

The Road to "I Do" in Mexico

20+ Days Before

Document Preparation: Scanned copies of passports, tourist cards, and divorce decrees (if applicable) sent to your coordinator.

3 Business Days Before

Arrival in Cancun: Couple must be in the country. Blood tests and health certificates are completed at the resort.

The Wedding Day

The Ceremony: Performed by the Judge. Requires 4 witnesses (we can provide them if needed).

2-4 Months After

Apostille Process: Your legal certificate is sent to the state capital for validation before being mailed to your home.

Expert Planning Advice

Tip 01

The Passport Rule

Ensure your passports are valid for at least 6 months after your wedding date to avoid entry issues at Mexican customs.

Tip 02

Witnesses

Legal weddings require 4 witnesses with valid IDs. If you're eloping, our staff can happily serve as your witnesses.

Tip 03

Name Changes

Most couples prefer symbolic ceremonies to simplify name change paperwork back home using their domestic certificate.

Tip 04

Stay Requirements

Plan to arrive at least 3 business days early for a legal wedding. Saturday and Sunday do not count toward this requirement.
