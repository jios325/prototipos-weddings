The Mayan Ceremony

A sacred blessing from one of the world's oldest civilizations

For over three thousand years, the Maya have cultivated a profound spiritual connection with the natural world, viewing the earth, sky, and sea as sacred vessels of divine energy. Choosing a Mayan ceremony is more than a cultural experience; it is a spiritual immersion that honors the wisdom of ancestors. As the copal smoke rises and the conch shell echoes across the turquoise waves, your union is woven into the eternal tapestry of time and nature.

Guided by a Mayan Priest

Each ceremony is officiated by an authentic Mayan Shaman, who serves as the bridge between the physical world and the spiritual realm. Dressed in traditional white regalia, they channel ancestral energy through sacred chants and prayers in the native Mayan tongue.

Honoring the Four Elements

The sacred altar represents the cardinal points and the four elements: Fire for passion, Water for purity, Earth for stability, and Wind for communication. Together, they create a balanced sanctuary for your vows to take root.

Seven Spiritual Directions

The Shaman invokes the seven directions: North, South, East, West, Above, Below, and the Center (the heart). This comprehensive blessing ensures your marriage is protected and supported by all forces of the universe.
